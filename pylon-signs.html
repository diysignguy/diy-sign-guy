Sign Types Explained

Pylon Pole Signs: Big Visibility, DIY Price

The pylon sign is the king of roadside visibility — a lighted cabinet mounted high on a single pole, readable from down the street or off the highway. Here's what they are, how they install, and how our 10" kit gets you one for a fraction of retail.

What Is a Pylon Sign?

The Sign Customers See From the Road

A pylon (or pole) sign is a freestanding sign mounted on a single steel pole, standing on its own out by the street. Gas stations, shopping centers, restaurants, and businesses set back from the road all rely on them — because a wall sign can't do its job if drivers can't see your wall.

Our 10" wide cabinet is purpose-built for pylon and single-pole mounting. The extra depth gives the cabinet the strength and presence a freestanding sign needs, with the same welded corners and Pre-Lit LED system as every kit we build.

Like all our kits, it's cut to your size, welded in our shop, and shipped nationwide in sections with the LEDs and power supply included.

Install Expectations

What a Pylon Install Involves

A pylon sign is the most involved install we offer — very doable for a contractor or an experienced DIYer, but go in with eyes open.

1

Check Local Requirements

Most cities require a sign permit for freestanding signs, and height/size limits vary. Check with your local permitting office before you order — we'll help you spec a cabinet that fits the rules.

2

Set the Pole

A pylon sign needs a steel pole set in a concrete footing. Pole and concrete are sourced locally (shipping steel pipe nationwide doesn't make sense for anyone).

3

Mount the Cabinet

Your 10" cabinet arrives in sections, assembles on site, and mounts to the pole. Plan on equipment or extra hands for the lift — this is a two-person-plus job.

4

Power It Up

Run power to the sign and connect the included 12V supply. Many owners have an electrician handle the final hookup — it's a small job once the sign is up.
